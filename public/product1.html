Description

I was feeling frustrated as I headed to my psychologist's office for what I believe was my sixth session.
It was hot, and I was gliding through the city streets on my skateboard, my mind weighed down.
At home, I had just finished the base of a new artwork, but I couldn’t grasp its meaning, nor could I understand why my mind and hands had created something so mysterious.
When I arrived at the studio, overheated, I exchanged cordial greetings and rolled up my sleeves.
The psychologist noticed my tattoos: a snake with two heads. She asked me what it represented.

I replied, "In life, I’ve learned to live with who I am and who I need to be in society.
The ink on my skin is my art, like ghosts moving in the shadows of my body, two minds battling to take hold of reality."
We continued talking about my tattoos, one by one, until she noticed my frustration.

"What’s going on?" she asked.

"I started a piece today, but I can't figure out its meaning.
It feels like my emotions and my expressive abilities are being held back by the limits I’ve imposed on myself, to avoid getting hurt by the outside world."

I described the artwork to her: a sheet of das covering woven sheets of paper, writings, words, and dates.
My thoughts, as if suffocated under a blanket. A blanket that won’t let them out.

"An oppressive blanket," she responded.

"Yes, but it should only protect me, not suffocate me," I immediately replied.
"It’s like I’ve fallen into a pool of oil, struggling and screaming, but unable to get out."

There was a long silence, which she abruptly broke: "You can’t do that!
You can’t put everything you feel under a blanket.
There is no emotion without its negative counterpart, no passion without hate…"

I continued, "I’ve put all of myself under a blanket soaked in oil, and now I can’t breathe."

That day, I decided to remove the blanket, and this is what came of it.
I thought a life without risks would be better, but I’ve discovered that a life without risk is not life.
